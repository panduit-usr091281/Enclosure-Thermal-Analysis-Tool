Calculation Reference

Formulas, constants, and methodology used by this calculator

1. Passive Heat Dissipation

Q = k × Aeff × ΔT
QTotal heat dissipated by enclosure (Watts)
kOverall thermal transmittance coefficient (W/m²·°C)
AeffEffective external surface area (m²)
ΔTTemperature rise above ambient = Tinternal − Tambient (°C)

2. Temperature Rise

ΔT = Q / (k × Aeff)

Rearranged from the heat dissipation equation. Gives the temperature rise inside the enclosure above the surrounding ambient air temperature for a given heat load.

3. Maximum Ambient Temperature

Tamb(max) = Tinternal(max) − ΔT

The highest ambient temperature at which the enclosure can maintain the internal temperature at or below the specified maximum, with no active cooling.

4. Maximum Heat Dissipation at 40°C Ambient

Qmax = k × Aeff × (Tinternal(max) − 40)

The maximum watts the enclosure can passively dissipate when the ambient is at the standard baseline of 40°C (104°F).

5. Heat from Equipment Efficiency

Qheat = Ptotal × (1 − η / 100)
PtotalTotal equipment power consumption (Watts)
ηEquipment efficiency (%); e.g. 96% for typical network switches
QheatWaste heat generated (Watts)

Example: A 300W device at 96% efficiency produces 300 × (1 − 0.96) = 12W of heat.

6. Solar Heat Gain (Direct Sunlight)

Qsolar = α × I × Aexposed
αSolar absorptivity of surface (0 to 1)
ISolar irradiance = 1000 W/m² (standard peak)
AexposedSun-exposed area = Top + Largest vertical face (m²)
ColorαExamples
Black / Dark0.9Black, dark gray, dark blue
Medium0.7Gray, olive, brown
Light0.5Beige, light gray, tan
White / Reflective0.3White, polished aluminum

Solar gain is added to equipment heat load: Qtotal = Qequipment + Qsolar. Based on worst-case peak solar irradiance at sea level.

7. Effective Surface Area by Mounting Type

MountingFormulaNotes
Wall MountedFront + ½Back + Left + Right + TopBack panel against wall (50% effective); bottom excluded
Floor StandingFront + ½Back + Left + Right + TopBack near wall (50% effective); bottom on floor excluded
Free StandingFront + Back + Left + Right + TopAll sides exposed; bottom excluded
Pole MountedFront + Back + Left + Right + TopAll sides exposed; bottom excluded

Individual panel areas: Front/Back = H×W, Left/Right = H×D, Top/Bottom = W×D.

8. Thermal Transmittance Coefficients (k)

Materialk (W/m²·°C)Notes
Painted Mild Steel5.5Standard painted carbon steel enclosure
Stainless Steel (304)5.2Typical brushed/passivated finish
Aluminum (Painted)5.5Paint dominates emissivity; same as painted steel
Fiberglass (GRP)3.4Glass-reinforced polyester, lower conductivity
Polycarbonate2.5Overall U-value including convection & radiation

These are overall heat transfer coefficients (U-values), not material thermal conductivity. They account for convection on both surfaces, radiation, and wall conduction in series.

9. Forced Airflow (Fan CFM)

CFM = (1.76 × Q) / ΔT
CFMRequired airflow in cubic feet per minute
QTotal heat load to be removed by forced air (Watts)
ΔTAllowable temperature rise = Tinternal(max) − Tambient (°C)
1.76Conversion constant: 1 / (volumetric heat capacity of air per CFM·°C) ≈ 1 / 0.569

Assumes ambient air is drawn through the enclosure. Baseline ambient used for CFM sizing: 40°C (104°F).

10. Fan with Passive Cooling Combined

Tinternal = Tambient + Q / (k × Aeff + CFM / 1.76)

When a fan is present, the effective thermal conductance increases by CFM/1.76 (W/°C). This combined formula is used for the fan cooling analysis charts and tables.

11. Unit Conversions

°F → °C(°F − 32) × 5/9
°C → °F°C × 9/5 + 32
mm → m÷ 1000
in → m× 0.0254
cm → m÷ 100
Disclaimer: These calculations are based on simplified steady-state models and standard empirical coefficients. Actual thermal performance may vary due to solar loading, internal airflow patterns, gasket condition, altitude, humidity, and other environmental factors. Results should be used as engineering estimates, not as a substitute for detailed thermal analysis or testing.
